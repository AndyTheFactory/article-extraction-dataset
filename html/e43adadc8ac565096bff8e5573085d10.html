‘Revenge Spending’ Drives Ballooning US Credit Card Balances

Americans have spent down their savings and racked up debt as the economy hums along, but experts are pondering whether consumers can keep going. 

Have a confidential tip for our reporters? Get in Touch

By Paulina Cachero
December 22, 2023 at 9:00 AM EST
Updated on
December 22, 2023 at 1:28 PM EST

American consumers continued to splurge in 2023, boosting the economy with revenge travel, Taylor Swift tickets and expensive restaurant meals. But a lot of it was funded with debt.

Credit card balances in the US increased by about 4.7% to $48 billion in the third quarter alone, pushing the total to $1.08 trillion, according to the New York Federal Reserve. It’s the highest total in data going back to 2003, and that was before the holiday shopping season started in earnest. The bills are mounting at time when the average annual percentage rate, or APR, has soared north of 20% to the highest on record.
