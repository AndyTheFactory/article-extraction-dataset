A Festivus for the rest of us! Get your aluminum poles and meatloaves ready

Kaitlyn McCormick   Cherry Hill Courier-Post

Get your aluminum pole, prep your meatloaf and your grievances and be ready to out-perform the head of your household.

That's right. It's time for Festivus.

What is Festivus?

For those who may not recall the “Seinfeld” episode that put this secular holiday on the map (calendar?) or for those who simply weren't alive during its 1997 airing (we see you Gen-Z), Festivus is a unique celebration that takes place on Dec. 23.

The holiday was a main plot line of the episode "The Strike."

How do you celebrate Festivus?

The art of celebrating Festivus comes down to a perfected itinerary of traditions.

First, make sure your decorations are in order. No need for all of the typical frivolities of Christmas. Just an unadorned aluminum pole will do. Tinsel is distracting after all.

Next, what ever will you eat? The Festivus feast typically includes some sort of meat dish. In Seinfeld, meatloaf served on a bed of lettuce was the winning choice.

This is when things get interesting.

During the Festivus dinner, be sure to have your notes ready for the Airing of the Grievances. Take turns airing every gripe, grimace and complaint you may have about family, friends or otherwise.

Be sure to stay sprightly for the feats of strength. Immediately after dinner, the head of the household will pick one person to challenge in a wrestling match. Once the head of household has been pinned, Festivus concludes.
