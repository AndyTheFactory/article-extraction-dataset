      Bruce Dern explains his revolutionary acting method

      Dern joins Q's Tom Power to talk about being trained in the esteemed Actors Studio in New York, his method of acting called the Dernsie, and the time he pulled a Dernsie in a scene with Brad Pitt.

      Jack Nicholson coined Dern's style of acting 'the Dernsie'

      CBC Arts · Posted: Dec 20, 2023 10:27 AM EST | Last Updated: December 21, 2023

      Social Sharing

      Q12:40Bruce Dern explains his revolutionary acting method

      Quentin Tarantino has called Bruce Dern an "acting national treasure" — and it's easy to see why. Dern, 87, has left a huge mark on the field.

      Before heading to Los Angeles to try his hand at the movie business, Dern learned his trade at the renowned Actors Studio in New York, counting legends like Paul Newman among his peers.

      He's starred in films like Coming Home and Nebraska, led cult classics like They Shoot Horses, Don't They? and Silent Running, worked with acclaimed directors such as Alfred Hitchcock, Francis Ford Coppola and Alexander Payne, and in recent years, he's sprung up in the Tarantino movies Django Unchained, The Hateful Eight and Once Upon a Time... in Hollywood. 

      • Laura Dern's role in The Son continues her family's legacy of method acting

      Dern got his star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame the same day as his daughter, Laura Dern, got hers. He even has a style of acting named after him, the Dernsie, coined by his acting buddy Jack Nicholson. It references his habit of ad-libbing in scenes. 

      Dern's latest film is a Canadian prairie gothic drama called Hands That Bind. He joined Q's Tom Power to talk about it and reflect on his incredible life in acting.
