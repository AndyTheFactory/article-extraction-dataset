You feed him ‘gabagool’ and take him to therapy. It’s a Tony Soprano Tamagotchi.

  • Updated: Feb. 13, 2024, 11:31 p.m.|
  • Published: Feb. 12, 2024, 7:00 p.m.
Subscribers can gift articles to anyone
By
  • Jeremy Schneider | NJ Advance Media For NJ.com

One of the main themes of HBO’s “Sopranos” is the trauma Tony Soprano’s parents inflicted on the main character during his childhood. Livia and Johnny Soprano certainly did a number on Tony.

Now there’s a toy that will let you try to do a better job. Kind of.

DebonairHeads, an independent artist and toy maker based out of Texas, has created a Tony Soprano Tamagotchi-like toy. A Tonygotchi. The digital handheld toy is shaped like everyone’s favorite New Jersey mob boss and allows you to raise a tiny Tony on the screen. The game allows you to at dinner with the family, feed Tony “gabagool” (capicola), go to the bathroom, hit the strip club, beat up a rival mobster and even go to therapy with Dr. Melfi.

View this post on Instagram

A post shared by DebonairHeads (@debonairheads)

Long before iPhone games and Playstation 5s, Tamagotchis were among the most popular toys in the United States. Kids everywhere in the late 1990s and early 2000s had the little egg-shaped devices that allowed them to raise digital pets. While their popularity has faded, “Sopranos” is as popular as ever despite having been off the air for more than 16 years.

The bad news? A Tonygotchi will run you $750 dollars. The worse news? DebonairHeads made just five of them, and they already sold out.

To quote the man the game himself, what ya gonna do?

Still, we’re happy this exists — we think?
